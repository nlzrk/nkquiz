integration MC

Which technique is most appropriate for evaluating $\displaystyle\int x^2 e^x \, dx$?

correct

Let $u = x^2$, $dv = e^x dx$. Then $\int x^2 e^x dx = x^2 e^x - 2xe^x + 2e^x + C$.

linear algebra MC

Let $A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$. Which statement about eigenvalues is always true?

$$\det(A - \lambda I) = 0 \implies \lambda \text{ is an eigenvalue of } A$$

correct

The characteristic polynomial $\det(A - \lambda I)$ has degree $n$, giving $n$ eigenvalues (with multiplicity) over $\mathbb{C}$.

mechanics SA

A block of mass $m$ on a frictionless incline at angle $\theta$. Derive the acceleration along the slope.

incorrect

The component of gravity along the slope is $mg\sin\theta$, giving $a = g\sin\theta$. You may have used $\cos\theta$ — that is the component normal to the slope.
